VEXA is a productivity and time tracking tool I built for students who struggle with procrastination and spending too much time scrolling (something I personally struggled with too).
The goal was to build something beyond a normal to-do list. Instead of only showing what you need to do, VEXA helps you understand where your time actually goes and what patterns affect your productivity.

Why I built VEXA
A lot of productivity apps focus on planning your day, but I wanted to focus on reflection.
I wanted to answer questions like:
- Where am I actually spending my time?
- Am I following through on my goals?
- What habits are helping me or hurting me?

Features:
Productivity DNA:
Productivity DNA analyzes your past activity and creates a personal productivity profile based on your habits.

It looks at things like:
- your most productive hours
- average focus session length
- task completion patterns
- common distractions
- planning habits

Parallel Self:
Parallel Self shows how your current habits could affect your future.

It uses your average focus time and scrolling patterns to create simple projections:

- What happens if I continue my current habits?
- What happens if I improve by 30 minutes a day?

These are not predictions. They are just calculations based on your own data to show how small changes can add up.


Tasks:
Tasks allow you to track:
- deadlines
- priority
- estimated time
- why the task matters

Calender:
VEXA's calendar shows both future plans and past results.

Future:
- tests
- deadlines
- events

Past:
- completed tasks
- focus sessions
- daily summaries

Focus Mode:
A focus timer that records your work sessions and connects them with your tasks.

Scroll Tracker:
VEXA does not automatically track your apps or browser. Instead, users manually start and stop scrolling sessions.

After each session, they can reflect:
"Was this worth my time?"

Daily Reciept:
At the end of the day, VEXA creates a summary of:
- focus time
- scrolling time
- completed tasks
- biggest win
- biggest distraction
- tomorrow's priority

Analytics:
Simple charts showing:
- focus trends
- task completion
- time usage patterns

Terminal Center:
A terminal-style dashboard where users can explore their productivity data.

Examples:
why did I procrastinate this week?
VEXA analyzes the user's own logged data to find patterns.

Privacy:
Privacy was one of the biggest design decisions behind VEXA.

VEXA does not:
- track browser history
- monitor apps
- collect unnecessary personal information

Users choose what they want to log.
The app uses Supabase Row Level Security (RLS), meaning users can only access their own data.
